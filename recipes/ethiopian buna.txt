Ethiopian Buna (also known as Ethiopian coffee) is a traditional coffee ceremony that involves roasting, grinding, and brewing green coffee beans. Here's a recipe to make authentic Ethiopian Buna:
Ingredients:
1 cup green coffee beans (Ethiopian Yirgacheffe or Sidama)
1 tablespoon unsalted butter or niter kibbeh (clarified butter)
1 tablespoon sugar (or to taste)
1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom (optional)
Water
Equipment:
Jebena (traditional Ethiopian clay coffee pot) or a medium saucepan
Coffee roaster or skillet
Grinder or mortar and pestle
Incense (optional)
**Instructions:"
Step 1: Roasting
Preheat a skillet or coffee roaster over medium heat.
Add green coffee beans and roast, stirring frequently, until fragrant and dark brown (about 10-15 minutes).
Remove from heat.
Step 2: Grinding
Allow roasted beans to cool.
Grind beans using a grinder or mortar and pestle until medium-coarse.
Step 3: Brewing
Fill Jebena or saucepan with water.
Add ground coffee and stir.
Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
Simmer for 10-15 minutes or until coffee has reduced slightly.
Step 4: Serving
Strain coffee into small cups.
Add butter, sugar, and cardamom (if using).
Serve immediately.
**Traditional Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony:"
Gather guests and begin with burning incense.
Roast coffee beans in front of guests.
Grind and brew coffee.
Serve coffee in three rounds:
First round: "Abol" (beginning)
Second round: "Tola" (middle)
Third round: "Baraka" (blessing)
Discuss and socialize between rounds.
**Tips and Variations:"
Use traditional Ethiopian coffee beans for authentic flavor.
Adjust sugar and butter to taste.
Add a pinch of salt for a unique flavor.
Use a coffee filter or cheesecloth for a clearer brew.
