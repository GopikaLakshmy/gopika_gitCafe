Vietnamese Egg Coffee (Cà Phê Trứng) is a unique and delicious coffee drink originating from Hanoi, Vietnam. Here's a recipe:
Ingredients:
2 large egg yolks
1 tablespoon sweetened condensed milk
1 shot of strong brewed coffee (Vietnamese or French roast)
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
Ice cubes
Whipped cream (optional)
Instructions:
Brew a shot of strong coffee.
In a separate bowl, whisk egg yolks and sweetened condensed milk until smooth.
Add vanilla extract and whisk until combined.
Pour coffee into a glass filled with ice cubes.
Slowly pour egg mixture into coffee, holding back foam with a spoon.
Top with whipped cream (if using).
Traditional Vietnamese Method:
Brew coffee in a small metal filter (Phin).
In a bowl, whisk egg yolks and sweetened condensed milk.
Pour egg mixture into coffee, stirring constantly.
Tips and Variations:
Use room temperature eggs for better emulsification.
Adjust sweetness to taste.
Add a pinch of cinnamon or cocoa powder.
Use coconut milk or almond milk for a non-dairy version.

History and Cultural Significance:
Cà Phê Trứng originated in Hanoi during the French colonial era.
Egg coffee became popular as a substitute for milk, which was scarce.
Popular Variations:
Cà Phê Sữa Đá: coffee with condensed milk and ice.
Cà Phê Đen: black coffee.
Cà Phê Trứng Sữa: egg coffee with condensed milk.
Enjoy your delicious Vietnamese Egg Coffee!
